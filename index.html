New York State has the Lowest Rate of Veterans in the Country

By: MONIQUE MULIMA

December 16, 2021

The rates of veterans in the country greatly vary state by state. The national average is 8%, with Alaska having the highest rate and New York having the lowest rate at 12.2% and 4.6% respectively.

Factors Influencing Veteran Rates

There are numerous factors that influence veteran rates. One of these is location of military bases. Many of the states with high rates of veterans like Alaska and Virginia have a higher-than-average number of military bases and have multiple cities whose population relies on military jobs.

Another factor that influences veteran rates is the rate of homelessness among veterans. According to the U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs, veterans make up 8% of the U.S. homeless population. States in the West and South that have milder weather like, Washington, Oregon, Florida, Texas and California have higher rates of homeless veterans. Some studies have found that warmer areas generally tend to have higher rates of homelessness due to the impact of cold climates of homeless people. This could potentially cause a slight increase in veteran populations in these areas.
